    What is Psoriasis

    Psoriasis is a chronic, autoimmune disease that appears on the skin. It occurs when the immune system sends out faulty signals that speed up the growth cycle of skin cells. Psoriasis is not contagious.


    What does this mean for me

    This means that psoriasis is going to be with you for a while. Now depending on the type of psoriasis that you have there are different treatments and therapies. Don't worry though, even if you're stuck with psoriasis it won't affect you socially unless you let it.


    What are the different types of Psoriasis

    According to the National Psoriasis Foundation there are 5 types of psoriasis.

    Plaque psoriasis is the most common of all the types. About 80% of those with psoriasis have this type. You can recognize it by raised, inflamed, red lesions covered by a silvery white scale. It is typically found on the elbows, knees, scalp and lower back.
